🎓 Speedcubing Solve Methods Explained

👶 Layer By Layer (LBL) Method

This is the absolute best method for beginners. Instead of trying to solve the whole cube at once, you solve it one layer at a time from bottom to top:

  1. The White Cross: Solve 4 white edges on the bottom layer, matching them with their center colors.
  2. First Layer Corners: Insert the 4 white corners into their correct places. The bottom layer is now solved!
  3. Second Layer (Middle): Use simple algorithms like U R U' R' U' F' U F to insert the middle edges.
  4. OLL Edges (Yellow Cross): Make a yellow cross on top using F R U R' U' F'.
  5. OLL Corners (Yellow Face): Turn the whole top face yellow using the Sune algorithm: R U R' U R U2 R'.
  6. PLL Corners: Position the corners correctly using R' F R' B2 R F' R' B2 R2.
  7. PLL Edges: Swap the remaining edges to complete the cube using R U' R U R U R U' R' U' R2.

🚀 CFOP Method (Fridrich Method)

Used by almost all world-class speedcubers (including Cole!). It is a highly optimized version of LBL consisting of four steps:

  • Cross (C): Solve the bottom 4 cross edges. Advanced solvers do this in 8 moves or fewer, entirely in their head during inspection!
  • F2L (First Two Layers) (F): Solve a corner and a middle-layer edge pair at the same time, inserting them together. There are 41 cases, but it's mostly done intuitively.
  • OLL (Orient Last Layer) (O): Orient all last-layer pieces in one step. There are 57 algorithms.
  • PLL (Permute Last Layer) (P): Rearrange the oriented pieces of the last layer to solve the cube. There are 21 algorithms.

Fun Fact: Using CFOP, cubers can solve the Rubik's cube in under 5 seconds!

🧠 ZBLL Method (Expert / Pro)

ZBLL (Zborowski-Bruchem Last Layer) is one of the most advanced speedcubing methods in existence.

  • How it works: If you orient the top edges during the F2L step (e.g. using ZZ or ZBLS), ZBLL solves the entire last layer (OLL and PLL) in a single algorithm!
  • Why use it? It saves crucial fractions of a second by eliminating the pause between OLL and PLL.
  • The Catch: It requires memorizing 493 distinct algorithms. Cubers must inspect the top colors instantly to recognize which case they have.

Only the absolute best cubers in the world learn ZBLL to shave off their last remaining milliseconds.
