CFOP Method (Fridrich) ⚡

119 Algorithms Total
💡 Pro Tip: A cross could be solved in 8 moves or less every single time! Start planning your entire cross during the 15-second inspection.

PLL (Permute Last Layer) - 21 Cases

Rearrange the oriented pieces of the last layer to solve the entire cube!

OLL (Orient Last Layer) - 57 Cases

Orient all last layer pieces so the top face becomes a solid yellow color.

F2L (First Two Layers) - 41 Cases

Solve a corner and an edge piece together, and insert them into their slot simultaneously.

ZB Method (Zborowski-Bruchem) 🏆

795 Algorithms Total
🔥 Massive Database: ZB consists of 493 ZBLL algorithms and 302 ZBLS algorithms in a total of 795 algorithms for the ZB method!

ZBLL (ZB Last Layer) - 493 Cases

If the last layer cross edges are already oriented, ZBLL solves the entire last layer (OLL and PLL) in one single algorithm!

ZBLS (ZB Last Slot) - 302 Cases

Solves the final F2L slot AND orients all 4 top-layer edges at the same time, preparing the cube perfectly for ZBLL!

Roux Method 💡

Blockbuilding & LSE

The Roux method is a highly efficient speedcubing method that uses fewer moves than CFOP and does not rely heavily on algorithms, focusing instead on blockbuilding and M-slice moves.

Roux Solve Workflow:

1. First Block (FB)

Build a 1x2x3 block on the left side of the cube. This is done entirely intuitively.

2. Second Block (SB)

Build another 1x2x3 block on the right side of the cube, leaving the M (middle) and U (up) slices free. This is also intuitive.

3. CMLL (42 Algorithms)

Solves the corners of the last layer (orientation and permutation) while preserving the two side blocks. If corners are already solved, skip this step.

4. LSE (Last Six Edges)

Solves the remaining six edges (four on top, two on bottom) using only U and M moves. LSE is divided into three sub-steps: Edge Orientation (EO), LR Edges, and Mid-slice Edges.

ZZ Method (EOLine) 🎯

No Rotations

The ZZ method orientates all edges at the very beginning of the solve. This eliminates cube rotations entirely, leading to extremely fast turning speeds and ergonomics.

ZZ Solve Workflow:

1. EOLine (Edge Orientation + Line)

Orient all 12 edges on the cube, and place the DF and DB edges to form a "line" on the bottom layer. After this step, all remaining turns can be done using only R, L, and U moves (no rotations!).

2. ZZ F2L (Blockbuilding)

Build the left and right 2x2x3 blocks on both sides of the line. Since all edges are oriented, you can do this without rotating the cube.

3. COLL (42 Algorithms)

Solves the orientation and permutation of the last layer corners, leaving only the four edges to be permuted (EPLL). E.g., if you get a COLL skip, you go straight to EPLL.

4. EPLL (Edge Permutation)

Solve the final 4 edges of the last layer using simple PLL algorithms (U-perm, H-perm, Z-perm).
